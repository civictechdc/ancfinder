ANC Brigade: Strengthening Hyper-Local Government

Who are we?

ANC Brigade is a project of Code for DC, which is a Code for America Brigade. We’re volunteers interested in using technology and open data to improve our civic lives.

We welcome your feedback and ideas: tweet @leahbannon or email us at contactus@ancbrigade.com.

Our goal is to strengthen DC's unique hyper-local level of government called Advisory Neighborhood Commissions (ANCs). This is a work in progress.

This is a work in progress

Our goals are evolving as we get to know the ANC system better and hear from DC residents and ANC commissioners about their needs.

At a high level, our goals include:

  • Educate the public about DC’s unique ANC system.
  • Inspire DC residents to get involved in their ANC.
  • Help residents find their ANC, its meetings, and their commissioner.
  • Provide tools for ANC commissioners to connect with their constituents and easily publish official records.
  • Promote transparency and accountability in the ANC system.

Come to our next meeting if you would like to get involved.

  • Search online for ANC websites and social media and add it to this google doc.
  • Submit data or info about ANCs here.

What's next?

We’re working on the following tasks.

  • What are the laws regarding ANCs in the DC code?
  • How can we best explain their authority and how they have exercised it?
  • Gathering a list of existing ANC websites, social media, etc, in this google doc.
  • Tracking news about ANC decisions and spending.

And we are thinking about:

  • Determine what are all of the different sorts of documents produced by ANCs.
  • Investigate what tools would be useful for commissioners (blog, CMS, wiki, etc.).
  • Recruiting liaisons in each ANC to help record and share information about their ANC's activities.
